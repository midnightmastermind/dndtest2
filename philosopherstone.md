# 1️⃣ Nigredo → NOTICE (CBT Awareness Phase)

This is not “suffer poetically.”
It’s: observe without spiraling.

When you feel:

* Exhausted
* Meaningless
* Unmotivated
* Ashamed
* Overwhelmed

Instead of solving it, do this:

### Step 1: Name the Thought

CBT style.

> “I’m having the thought that I’ve lost my spark.”

Not:

> “I’ve lost my spark.”

That small shift creates space.

### Step 2: Separate Facts from Story

Write two columns:

Facts:

* I feel tired.
* I haven’t felt excited lately.

Story:

* I’m broken.
* I’ll never get it back.
* Something is wrong with me.

Nigredo is just noticing distortion.

You’re not fixing it yet.
You’re preventing the spiral.

That’s it.

---

# 2️⃣ Albedo → CLEAN (CBT/DBT Cognitive Work)

Now we purify the distortion.

Pick one strong thought.

Example:

> “I can’t get excited about Portugal anymore, so something’s wrong.”

Now challenge it gently:

* Is this permanent?
* What evidence supports it?
* What evidence doesn’t?
* What would I say to a friend in this state?

Then replace with something realistic:

> “Excitement fluctuates. Burnout can numb anticipation.”

Not toxic positivity.
Just balanced cognition.

DBT version of this is “Check the Facts.”

This stage is boring.
It’s repetition.
But this is where your brain rewires.

---

# 3️⃣ Citrinitas → PRACTICE (Behavioral Activation)

Insight without action does nothing.

Now we do small embodied reps.

Not big goals.
Small ones.

Examples:

If motivation is low:

* 10 minute rule. Just start.
* 1 Pomodoro only.
* One push-up.
* One paragraph of code.
* One song verse.

You are teaching your nervous system:

> “We move even when mood is low.”

This is classic behavioral activation.

You don’t wait for inspiration.
You create motion.

This is where Wu Wei actually starts making sense:
Action without emotional drama.

# 4️⃣ Rubedo → INTEGRATE (DBT Wise Mind)

Now we combine opposites.

DBT calls this **Wise Mind**:

* Emotional Mind
* Rational Mind
* Integrated Mind

Example:

Emotional Mind:

> “I feel like quitting everything.”

Rational Mind:

> “That’s dramatic and illogical.”

Wise Mind:

> “I’m overwhelmed. I don’t need to quit. I need rest and structure.”

Rubedo isn’t being happy.
It’s being coherent.

You can:

* Want comfort
* Want discipline
* Feel shame
* Feel ambition
* Feel sexual complexity
* Feel spiritual curiosity

Without splitting yourself into good/bad halves.

Integration means:

> “All of this is me. I choose my actions consciously.”
